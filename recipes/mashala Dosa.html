Masala Dosa

Masala Dosa is a popular South Indian dish that consists of a crispy and thin fermented rice and lentil crepe (dosa) filled with a spiced potato filling. It is typically served with coconut chutney and sambar. Here's a recipe to make Masala Dosa:

01

Ingredients: For Dosa Batter:

  • 1 cup regular rice (short or medium grain)
  • 1 cup parboiled rice (idli rice) or sona masuri rice
  • 1/2 cup split black gram lentils (urad dal)
  • 1/2 teaspoon fenugreek seeds (methi seeds)
  • Water, as needed for soaking and grinding
  • Salt to taste

Ingredients: For Potato Masala (Filling):

  • 3 large potatoes, boiled and mashed
  • 1 medium-sized onion, finely chopped
  • 2-3 green chilies, finely chopped (adjust to your spice preference)
  • 1/2 teaspoon mustard seeds
  • 1/2 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • A pinch of asafoetida (hing)
  • 1 sprig curry leaves
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon red chili powder (adjust to your spice preference)
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • Salt to taste
  • Fresh coriander leaves for garnish

02

Instructions: For Dosa Batter:

  1. Wash the regular rice, parboiled rice, and urad dal separately. Add fenugreek seeds to the urad dal while washing.
  2. Soak the regular rice and parboiled rice together in one bowl and the urad dal with fenugreek seeds in another bowl. Soak them in water for at least 4-6 hours.
  3. After soaking, drain the water and grind the urad dal and fenugreek seeds to a smooth and fluffy batter. Use as little water as possible while grinding. Transfer the batter to a large container.
  4. Now, grind the rice with some water to a fine and smooth batter. Add this to the container with the urad dal batter.
  5. Mix both batters together thoroughly. Add salt and mix again. The batter should be of pouring consistency. Cover the container and let it ferment overnight or for about 8-12 hours in a warm place.

Instructions: For Potato Masala (Filling):

  1. Heat oil in a pan or a kadhai over medium heat. Add mustard seeds and let them splutter.
  2. Add cumin seeds, asafoetida, and curry leaves. Stir for a few seconds.
  3. Add finely chopped onions and green chilies. Saut√© until the onions become translucent.
  4. Add turmeric powder, red chili powder, and salt. Mix well.
  5. Add the boiled and mashed potatoes to the pan. Stir everything together until the masala is well combined. Cook for a few minutes to let the flavors meld. You can add a little water to achieve your desired consistency.
  6. Turn off the heat and garnish the potato masala with freshly chopped coriander leaves.

Instructions: Making Masala Dosa:

  1. Heat a flat non-stick or cast-iron griddle (tawa) over medium heat. Sprinkle a few drops of water on the tawa; if it sizzles, it's ready.
  2. Take a ladleful of dosa batter and pour it onto the center of the tawa. Using the back of the ladle, spread the batter in a circular motion to form a thin and even dosa.
  3. Drizzle a little oil or ghee around the edges of the dosa and on top of it. Cook until the dosa turns golden brown and crispy.
  4. Place a portion of the potato masala filling in the center of the dosa. Fold the dosa in half to cover the filling.
  5. Gently remove the Masala Dosa from the tawa and serve hot with coconut chutney and sambar.

03

Serving:

Enjoy the delicious and crispy Masala Dosa as a breakfast or a snack!
