Chole Bhature

Chole Bhature is a popular North Indian dish consisting of spicy chickpea curry (chole) served with puffy fried bread (bhature). It's a delightful combination loved by many. Here's a recipe to make Chole Bhature:

01

Ingredients: For Chole (Chickpea Curry):

  • 1 cup dried chickpeas (garbanzo beans), soaked overnight
  • 2 medium-sized onions, finely chopped
  • 2 medium-sized tomatoes, pureed or finely chopped
  • 2-3 green chilies, finely chopped (adjust to your spice preference)
  • 1 tablespoon ginger-garlic paste
  • 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 1 teaspoon coriander powder
  • 1 teaspoon garam masala
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 teaspoon red chili powder (adjust to your spice preference)
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground black pepper
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 1 tablespoon ghee (clarified butter) - optional, but adds flavor
  • Salt to taste
  • Fresh coriander leaves for garnish

Ingredients: For Bhature (Fried Bread):

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour (maida)
  • 1/4 cup semolina (suji/rawa)
  • 1/2 cup plain yogurt
  • 1 teaspoon sugar
  • 1/2 teaspoon baking powder
  • 1/4 teaspoon baking soda
  • 1 tablespoon vegetable oil
  • Salt to taste
  • Water, as needed, to knead the dough
  • Oil for deep frying

02

Instructions: For Chole (Chickpea Curry):

  1. Drain the soaked chickpeas and rinse them under cold water. Cook the chickpeas in a pressure cooker or a large pot with enough water and some salt until they are tender. This usually takes about 15-20 minutes in the pressure cooker or around 45 minutes in a regular pot.
  2. Heat vegetable oil in a large pan or a deep skillet over medium heat. Add cumin seeds and let them splutter.
  3. Add finely chopped onions and saut√© until they turn golden brown.
  4. Add green chilies and ginger-garlic paste. Cook for a couple of minutes until the raw smell disappears.
  5. Add tomato puree or finely chopped tomatoes and cook until the oil starts to separate from the masala and the tomatoes are well-cooked.
  6. Now, add all the dry spices - coriander powder, garam masala, turmeric powder, red chili powder, black pepper, and salt. Mix well and cook for a few more minutes.
  7. Add the cooked chickpeas along with the water they were cooked in (or you can add water separately) to achieve the desired consistency. Simmer the chole for about 10-15 minutes, allowing the flavors to blend together. If needed, adjust the salt and spices according to your taste.
  8. Optionally, add ghee to enhance the flavor. Mix well and turn off the heat.
  9. Garnish the Chole with freshly chopped coriander leaves.

Instructions: For Bhature (Fried Bread):

  1. In a large mixing bowl, combine all-purpose flour, semolina, baking powder, baking soda, sugar, salt, and vegetable oil.
  2. Add yogurt and start kneading the dough. Gradually add water as needed and knead until you get a soft and smooth dough. The dough should be slightly sticky but manageable.
  3. Coat the dough with a little oil, cover it with a damp cloth, and let it rest for about 2 hours. This step helps to make the bhature soft and fluffy.
  4. After the resting period, divide the dough into small lemon-sized balls.
  5. Roll out each ball into a thin disc using a rolling pin. The bhature should be about 6-7 inches in diameter.
  6. Heat oil in a deep frying pan or a kadhai over medium-high heat.
  7. Carefully slide one bhature into the hot oil and lightly press it with a slotted spoon. This will help the bhature to puff up.
  8. Fry the bhature until they are golden brown on both sides.
  9. Remove the fried bhature from the oil and place them on paper towels to remove excess oil.

03

Serving:

Serve the hot and spicy Chole with the puffy Bhature, along with sliced onions, green chilies, and a side of pickle. Some people also enjoy it with yogurt (raita) to balance the spiciness. Enjoy your homemade Chole Bhature!
