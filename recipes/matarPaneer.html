Matar Paneer

Matar Paneer is a delicious and popular North Indian dish made with green peas and paneer (Indian cottage cheese) cooked in a flavorful tomato-based gravy. Here's a simple recipe to make Matar Paneer at home:

01

Ingredients:

  • 200g paneer, cubed
  • 1 cup green peas (fresh or frozen)
  • 2 medium-sized onions, finely chopped
  • 2 medium-sized tomatoes, pureed or finely chopped
  • 1 tablespoon ginger-garlic paste
  • 2-3 green chilies, slit (adjust to your spice preference)
  • 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 teaspoon red chili powder (adjust to your spice preference)
  • 1 teaspoon garam masala
  • 1/2 teaspoon cumin powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon coriander powder
  • 1/4 cup fresh cream (optional, for a richer gravy)
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil or ghee (clarified butter)
  • Salt to taste
  • Fresh coriander leaves for garnish

02

Instructions:

  1. Heat oil or ghee in a pan or a kadhai over medium heat. Add cumin seeds and let them splutter.
  2. Add chopped onions and saut√© until they turn golden brown.
  3. Add ginger-garlic paste and green chilies. Cook for a couple of minutes until the raw smell disappears.
  4. Add tomato puree or finely chopped tomatoes. Cook until the tomatoes are well-cooked and the oil starts to separate from the masala.
  5. Add turmeric powder, red chili powder, cumin powder, coriander powder, garam masala, and salt. Mix well and cook for a few more minutes to let the spices blend together.
  6. Add green peas and a little water to cook them. Cover the pan and cook until the peas are tender (if using frozen peas, they will cook quickly).
  7. Once the peas are cooked, add the paneer cubes to the gravy. If you prefer, you can lightly fry the paneer cubes in a separate pan before adding them to the gravy.
  8. Stir everything gently to coat the paneer and peas with the gravy. If the gravy seems too thick, you can add a little water to adjust the consistency.
  9. Optionally, add fresh cream to the gravy for a richer taste and creamier texture. Mix well and simmer for a couple of minutes.
  10. Turn off the heat and garnish the Matar Paneer with freshly chopped coriander leaves.

03

Serving:

Serve the delicious Matar Paneer with Indian bread like naan, roti, or paratha, or with steamed rice. It also pairs well with jeera rice (cumin rice). Enjoy the creamy and flavorful dish with your family and friends!

Note: Paneer is delicate, so handle it gently to prevent it from breaking while cooking. If you want a vibrant green color in the dish, you can blanch the green peas before adding them to the gravy. Also, feel free to adjust the spice levels and creaminess according to your preference.
